Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a beloved comfort food that's cherished for its crispy, golden-brown exterior and juicy, tender interior. This classic dish consists of bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces, often thighs and drumsticks, coated in a seasoned flour mixture and deep-fried until irresistibly crunchy. The seasoned flour blend adds a delightful mix of flavors, and the end result is a satisfying and flavorful treat that's perfect for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 Bone-in Chicken pieces
  • 1 Cup all-purpose Flour
  • 1 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Black Pepper
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Paprika
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Onion Powder
  • Vegetable Oil

Steps

  2. In a shallow dish or a plastic bag, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and dried thyme. Mix well.
  3. Dredge each chicken piece in the seasoned flour mixture, ensuring it's coated evenly. Shake off any excess flour.
  4. Carefully place the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil, one at a time. Be cautious to avoid overcrowding the skillet, which can reduce the oil temperature and affect the crispiness of the chicken.
  5. Fry the chicken for about 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the pieces are golden brown and the internal temperature reaches at least 165째F (74째C).
  6. Remove the fried chicken from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  7. Allow the fried chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving. This helps the chicken stay juicy and retain its crispy coating.
  8. Serve your simple fried chicken hot with your favorite side dishes, such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits. Enjoy!
